Sri Lankan Heritage

Day 3

Next we leave for the Anuradhapura to visit Sri Mahabodhi ,Ruwanweli Seya and other world’s famous ruins. Anuradhapura is the most celebrated of Sri Lanka's ancient ruined cities. This city is the repository of several priceless treasures of Buddhism and as a World Heritage site definitely a must to every visitor to Sri Lanka Then you will return back to Sigiriya and stay an overnight at the hotel.

Day 4

For the next destination on your cultural tour, we take you to another historical destination. You will be taken to Polonnaruwa to witness ancient ruins. Polonnaruwa is the ancient capital of Sri Lanka from the eleventh to thirteenth centuries and the repository of outstanding ruins, frescoes and enormous statues of reclining Buddhas. The ruins of the old city are on the shores of Lake Topawewa, man-made during the reign of a twelfth century king-a huge task, when one considers it was accomplished with nothing but manual labor. What remains of the ancient city itself is a cluster of palaces and temples contained within a rectangular city wall. The foundations of the royal palace and the king's audience hall are particularly well preserved. At leisure
